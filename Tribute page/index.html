Muhammad Ali

1942-2016

Biography

Muhammad Ali (1942-2016) was an influential heavyweight boxer and civil rights activist. Known for his dynamic boxing style and magnetic personality, Ali became a three-time world heavyweight champion. His refusal to be drafted into the Vietnam War and his outspoken advocacy for racial equality made him a symbol of strength and resilience. Despite his battle with Parkinson's disease later in life, Ali's legacy as "The Greatest" endures, inspiring millions with his athletic prowess and unwavering convictions.



Here's a timeline of Muhammad Ali's life:


  • 1942 - Born as Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. on January 17th in Louisville, Kentucky.
  • 1954 - Begins boxing training at the age of 12 after his bicycle is stolen and expresses a desire to confront the thief.
  • 1960 - Wins a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the Rome Olympics.
  • 1964 - Defeats Sonny Liston to become the world heavyweight champion at the age of 22.
  • 1966 - Refuses to be drafted into the United States military during the Vietnam War.
  • 1970 - The Supreme Court overturns Ali's conviction for draft evasion.
  • 1971 - Takes part in the "Fight of the Century" against Joe Frazier.
  • 1974 - Defeats George Foreman in the famous "Rumble in the Jungle" match.
  • 1975 - Has the legendary "Thrilla in Manila" bout against Joe Frazier.
  • 1980 - Retires from professional boxing.
  • 1984 - Diagnosed with Parkinson's disease.
  • 1996 - Lights the Olympic cauldron at the opening ceremony of the Atlanta Olympics.
  • 2005 - Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
  • 2016 - Muhammad Ali passes away on June 3rd at the age of 74 in Scottsdale, Arizona.
